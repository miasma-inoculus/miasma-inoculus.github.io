THE LAST PASSENGER

The collector moves through the cars, finding them all empty—save for a strange, lingering chill. In the very last car, he finds a man sitting perfectly still.

A station that doesn't exist.

The ticket is cold to the touch. It isn't for a destination; it's dated forty years in the past—the date of a famous rail disaster on this very line.

Vol. I · Issue No. 3 · May 11, 1887

THE CARTOGRAPHER'S WIFE

Elena notices that her husband isn't drawing land anymore; he is drawing her. But the anatomy is impossible—he’s sketching her with streets for veins and cathedrals for a heart.

The borders are bleeding.

She opens the door to the "new room" and finds herself standing on a vast, empty cliffside overlooking the Uncharted Coast. She has been mapped into existence.

Vol. I · Issue No. 4 · May 18, 1887

THE MIRROR AT THE END

The Silas in the mirror is now the one who is healthy and alive, stepping out into the bedroom. The real Silas is trapped behind the glass.

Looking out at his own life.

He is frozen in the moment of his final breath, a spectator to a stranger living his life.

Vol. I · Issue No. 5 · May 25, 1887

THE WATCHMAN'S HOUR

Every time the clock strikes the hour, a room in the house changes. He realizes he isn't just watching a house—he’s watching the house age in reverse.

The thirteenth hour strikes.

He is trapped in a loop of his own life, a young boy watching a man enter to begin the shift.

Vol. I · Issue No. 6 · June 01, 1887

THE GOVERNESS' LETTER

As she reads the letter, she realizes it contains instructions on how to "prepare" the children for a visitor who is not human.

Written by the children.

The letter wasn't for the previous governess—it was a detailed plan for her own replacement.
