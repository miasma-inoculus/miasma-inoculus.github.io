THE THIRTEENTH KNOCK

"The house didn't open. The house simply accepted."

Elias Thorne lived by the rhythm of the rain and the iron-clad rules of the old manor. Twelve knocks meant the postman. Twelve knocks meant the neighbor. Twelve knocks was the boundary of the living world.

But tonight, as the clock struck midnight and the fog pressed against the glass like a physical weight, the sequence didn't stop. After the twelfth echo died in the rafters, a final, heavy thud vibrated through the floorboards. The Thirteenth Knock.

It wasn’t a sound of bone on wood; it was the sound of a debt being called. Elias didn't reach for the handle. He didn't have to. The door didn't open—the house simply accepted that whatever was outside was now, irrevocably, inside.
